# Trading Strategies

## Overview

LOHI-TRADE runs three intraday strategies on NSE equities during market hours (9:30 AM – 3:10 PM IST). All strategies operate on 5-minute candles and share a common exit framework (ATR-based stops, trailing stops, forced square-off).

Every signal generated by The Soldier is filtered through The Commander's sentiment bias before reaching the execution layer.

---

## 1. Mean Reversion Strategy

Identifies oversold conditions where price has deviated significantly from its mean, expecting a snap-back.

### Entry Conditions (BUY)

All four conditions must be true simultaneously:

| Condition | Indicator | Threshold | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Oversold | RSI(14) | < 30 | Price momentum is extremely weak |
| Below lower band | Price < Lower BB(20, 2σ) | — | Price is statistically cheap |
| Volume spike | Volume > 1.5× 20-period avg | 1.5x | Confirms institutional participation |
| Above VWAP | Price > VWAP | — | Institutional support (buyers above avg price) |

### Exit Conditions

| Exit Type | Calculation | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Stop Loss | Entry − 1.5 × ATR(14) | Hard stop below entry |
| Target | Entry + 3.0 × ATR(14) | 2:1 risk-reward ratio |
| Trailing Stop | Moves up by 50% of unrealized profit | Locks in gains as price rises |
| Square-off | 3:15 PM IST | Forced close of all positions |

### Timeframe
- **Primary:** 5-minute candles
- **Indicators:** RSI(14), Bollinger Bands(20, 2σ), VWAP, ATR(14) — all on 5-minute data

### When It Works Best
- Choppy, range-bound markets
- High-volume stocks with strong mean-reverting tendencies
- Days without strong directional news flow

## 2. Trend Following Strategy

Captures sustained directional moves by entering when multiple trend indicators align.

### Entry Conditions (BUY)

All five conditions must be true simultaneously:

| Condition | Indicator | Threshold | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| EMA crossover | EMA(9) > EMA(21) | Crossover | Short-term trend is up |
| MACD positive | MACD line > 0 | — | Medium-term momentum is bullish |
| MACD rising | MACD histogram increasing | — | Momentum is accelerating |
| Above VWAP | Price > VWAP | — | Institutional support |
| Supertrend bullish | Supertrend(7, 3) direction = 1 | — | Trend confirmation |
| Volume confirmation | Volume > 20-period average | 1.0x | Adequate participation |

### Exit Conditions

| Exit Type | Calculation | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Stop Loss | Entry − 1.5 × ATR(14) | Hard stop below entry |
| Target | Entry + 3.0 × ATR(14) | 2:1 risk-reward ratio |
| Trailing Stop | Moves up by 50% of unrealized profit | Locks in gains |
| Square-off | 3:15 PM IST | Forced close of all positions |

### Timeframe
- **Primary:** 5-minute candles
- **Indicators:** EMA(9), EMA(21), MACD(12, 26, 9), VWAP, Supertrend(7, 3), ATR(14)

### When It Works Best
- Strong trending days (gap-up/gap-down opens)
- Sector-wide momentum moves
- Days with clear directional news catalysts

## 3. Opening Range Breakout (ORB)

Trades the breakout from the first 15-minute trading range, a well-known intraday pattern in Indian markets.

### Opening Range Definition
- **Range period:** 9:15 AM – 9:30 AM IST (first 15 minutes)
- **Range high:** Highest price during range period
- **Range low:** Lowest price during range period

### Entry Conditions

| Direction | Condition | Additional Filters |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **BUY** | Price breaks above range high + 0.1% buffer | Volume > 2× average at breakout candle |
| **SELL** | Price breaks below range low − 0.1% buffer | Volume > 2× average at breakout candle |

- **Active window:** 9:30 AM – 10:30 AM IST only (breakouts after 10:30 are ignored)
- **Breakout buffer:** 0.1% beyond range boundary to filter false breakouts

### Exit Conditions

| Exit Type | Calculation | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Stop Loss | Opposite end of opening range | If long, SL = range low; if short, SL = range high |
| Target | 2× range width from entry | Symmetrical target based on range size |
| Trailing Stop | Moves by 50% of unrealized profit | Locks in gains |
| Square-off | 3:15 PM IST | Forced close of all positions |

### Timeframe
- **Primary:** 5-minute candles (with 1-minute candles for range calculation)
- **Indicators:** Volume, ATR(14) for position sizing

### When It Works Best
- High-volatility open days
- Gap opens with follow-through
- Days with pre-market news catalysts

## Signal Filtering — The Commander's Bias

All signals from all three strategies pass through The Commander's sentiment bias filter before execution. This is RMS check #9.

### Filtering Rules

| Signal Direction | Bias | Result |
|-----------------|------|--------|
| BUY | BULLISH | ✅ Allowed (confluence) |
| BUY | NEUTRAL | ✅ Allowed |
| BUY | BEARISH | ❌ Rejected |
| SELL | BEARISH | ✅ Allowed (confluence) |
| SELL | NEUTRAL | ✅ Allowed |
| SELL | BULLISH | ❌ Rejected |

### How Bias Is Calculated
1. Financial news articles are scored by FinBERT ONNX (range: -1 to +1)
2. Scores are aggregated per symbol with exponential time decay (half-life: 4 hours)
3. Aggregated score > +0.2 → **BULLISH**
4. Aggregated score < −0.2 → **BEARISH**
5. Otherwise → **NEUTRAL**

## Risk Management (Common to All Strategies)

### Per-Trade Risk
- **Risk per trade:** 1% of total capital
- **Position sizing formula:** `quantity = (capital × 0.01) / (entry_price − stop_loss_price)`
- **Maximum position size:** 20% of total capital

### Portfolio-Level Limits
| Limit | Value | Enforced By |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Max open positions | 5 | RMS check #4 |
| Max orders per day | 20 | RMS check #6 |
| Max daily loss | 2% of capital | RMS check #3 + Kill Switch |
| Post-loss cooldown | 5 minutes | RMS check #7 |
| Volatility guard | Nifty > 2% drop in 10 min | RMS check #8 |

### Trailing Stop Mechanism
Once a position is in profit, the trailing stop activates:
1. Calculate unrealized profit: `current_price − entry_price`
2. New stop = `entry_price + (unrealized_profit × 0.50)`
3. Stop only moves up (for longs), never down
4. If price hits trailing stop → position closed at market

### Forced Square-Off
All open positions are closed at **3:15 PM IST**, regardless of P&L. This is 15 minutes before market close to ensure fills.

## Extended TA Strategies

The following 10 strategies are derived from classical technical analysis patterns and are integrated into the signal pipeline alongside the original 3 strategies. All signals pass through the same ML filter and Commander bias check.

---

### 4. VWAP Bounce Strategy

Trades bounces off VWAP, which acts as dynamic institutional support/resistance.

| Condition | Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Near VWAP | Price within 0.3% of VWAP | Proximity filter |
| Neutral RSI | RSI(14) between 40-60 | Not overbought/oversold |
| Volume | Volume > 1.2× average | Participation confirmation |
| Candle direction | Bullish/bearish candle body | Bounce confirmation |

- BUY: bullish candle bouncing off VWAP from below
- SELL: bearish candle rejecting VWAP from above

---

### 5. Stochastic + RSI Combo Strategy

Dual oscillator confirmation for oversold/overbought entries.

| Condition | Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Stochastic oversold | %K < 20 | Deep oversold |
| RSI oversold | RSI(14) < 35 | Momentum confirmation |
| %K cross | %K > %D | Bullish crossover |

- BUY: both oscillators oversold + %K crossing above %D
- SELL: both overbought + %K crossing below %D

---

### 6. ADX Trend Strength Strategy

Only enters when ADX confirms a strong trend with directional index crossover.

| Condition | Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Strong trend | ADX(14) > 25 | Trend strength filter |
| Direction | +DI > -DI (BUY) or -DI > +DI (SELL) | DI crossover |
| VWAP filter | Price vs VWAP | Institutional alignment |

---

### 7. Bollinger Band Squeeze Strategy

Detects volatility compression (narrow bands) and trades the breakout.

| Condition | Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Squeeze | BB width < 2% of middle | Low volatility |
| Breakout | Price > BB upper (BUY) or < BB lower (SELL) | Direction |
| Volume | Volume > 1.5× average | Breakout confirmation |

---

### 8. Pivot Point Strategy

Classic pivot point support/resistance bounce trading.

| Condition | Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Near S1/R1 | Price within 0.2% of pivot level | Proximity |
| RSI filter | RSI < 40 at S1 (BUY), RSI > 60 at R1 (SELL) | Momentum |
| Candle body | Bullish at support, bearish at resistance | Bounce |

---

### 9. Ichimoku Cloud Strategy

Full Ichimoku Kinko Hyo system for trend identification.

| Condition | Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Cloud position | Price above/below Kumo cloud | Trend direction |
| TK cross | Tenkan-sen vs Kijun-sen | Signal line cross |
| VWAP | Price vs VWAP | Institutional confirmation |

---

### 10. MACD Divergence Strategy

Detects price/momentum divergence for reversal entries.

| Condition | Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Price divergence | Lower lows (price) vs higher lows (MACD) | Bullish div |
| MACD confirmation | Histogram turning positive | Momentum shift |
| RSI filter | RSI < 45 for BUY, > 55 for SELL | Optional confirmation |

---

### 11. Parabolic SAR + EMA Trend Strategy

SAR direction combined with EMA stack alignment for trend continuation.

| Condition | Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| SAR direction | Parabolic SAR bullish/bearish | Primary signal |
| EMA alignment | EMA9 > EMA21 > EMA50 (BUY) | Trend stack |
| Volume | Volume > average | Participation |
| Stop loss | SAR value itself | Dynamic trailing stop |

---

### 12. Volume Breakout Strategy

Enters on extreme volume spikes confirming price breakouts.

| Condition | Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Volume spike | Volume > 2.5× average | Extreme participation |
| Price breakout | Close > 20-candle high (BUY) | Range breakout |
| MACD filter | MACD > 0 (BUY), < 0 (SELL) | Momentum alignment |

---

### 13. Multi-Timeframe Momentum Confluence

Counts how many indicators agree on direction; enters when confluence is high.

Scores 10 indicators: EMA cross, Supertrend, MACD, RSI, VWAP, ADX+DI, Stochastic, SAR, Ichimoku TK, CCI. Requires 5+ aligned for entry.

## ML Integration with Extended Strategies

All 13 strategies feed into the ML filter (GradientBoostingClassifier). The feature vector has been expanded from 16 to 29 features to include:
- Stochastic zone, ADX strength, DI crossover
- Williams %R zone, CCI zone, MFI zone
- Parabolic SAR direction, EMA trend alignment
- Ichimoku cloud position, TK cross
- Pivot position relative to ATR
- Confluence score (bullish indicator count / 10)

The ML model learns which strategy signals are profitable under which market conditions, effectively creating an adaptive meta-strategy that weights the 13 base strategies dynamically.
